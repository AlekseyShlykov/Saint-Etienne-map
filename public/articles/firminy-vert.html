Firminy-Vert

Firminy-Vert is one of the largest ensembles of Le Corbusier’s work in Europe. Built from the 1960s onwards in the town of Firminy, near Saint-Étienne, it includes a cultural centre (Maison de la Culture), a stadium, a housing unit (Unité d’habitation), and the famous Saint-Pierre church, completed after the architect’s death.

The site reflects Le Corbusier’s vision of “vertical garden city” and modernist urban planning. The concrete structures and bold forms have become a pilgrimage for architecture enthusiasts.

Guided tours are available to discover the history and design of each building. The church, with its truncated cone and play of light, is particularly striking.
