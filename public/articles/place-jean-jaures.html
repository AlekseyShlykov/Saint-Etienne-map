Place Jean Jaurès

Place Jean Jaurès is the main square of Saint-Étienne and has long been the political and commercial heart of the city. Surrounded by shops, cafés, and the former Bourse du Travail, it hosts markets and events throughout the year.

The square is named after Jean Jaurès, the French socialist leader and historian. A statue and a tram stop make it a central meeting point for residents and visitors alike.

From here you can easily reach the Musée d’Art et d’Industrie, the cathedral, and the main pedestrian streets. The area is fully pedestrianised on market days, offering a lively atmosphere.

Whether you come for the market, a coffee, or simply to people-watch, Place Jean Jaurès remains an essential stop on any visit to Saint-Étienne.
