Rive-de-Gier Canal

The canal from Givors to Rive-de-Gier was built in the 19th century to transport coal from the Saint-Étienne basin to the Rhône. Today it is a key element of the industrial heritage of the Gier valley and a pleasant setting for walks and cycling.

The towpath has been converted into a greenway (Voie verte), linking several towns along the canal. Rive-de-Gier itself offers museums and remnants of the mining and metallurgical past.

Whether you are interested in industrial history or simply want a peaceful walk along the water, the Rive-de-Gier canal and its surroundings are well worth a visit.
